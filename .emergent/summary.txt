The user initially requested a Fiscal Data Capture component for their Point of Sale system, which should appear for specific fiscal types (B01, B14, B15), validate the RNC/Cédula, search for existing clients, and persist new ones.

Subsequently, the user requested a major architectural change to implement a Business Date (Jornada de Trabajo) system. This system decouples the accounting date from the calendar date. Key requirements include:
1.  **Manual Day Open/Close:** The business date is set upon Apertura de Día and remains fixed until a Cierre de Día is performed by an authorized user. Transactions are recorded against this business date.
2.  **Dual Timestamps:** Every transaction must store both the real timestamp and the active business date.
3.  **Workflow Enforcement:** The system must block sales if no business day is open. The flow is: Open Day -> Open Shift -> Sales -> Close Shift (Report X) -> Close Day (Report Z).
4.  **Detailed Reporting (X/Z):** Generate reports with breakdowns by payment method, category, discounts, and a final cash summary.
5.  **Report Synchronization:** All administrative reports must be filterable by the Business Date.
6.  **Audit Security:** Restrict visibility of user PINs to only the 'Admin' role.
7.  **DGII Reports:** Clarified requirements for fiscal reports 607 (including B04 Notes of Credit affecting other invoices) and 608 (annulled NCFs). Implementation of 608 is currently on hold per user request.
